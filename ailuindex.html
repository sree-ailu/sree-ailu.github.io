Frameworks & Scholarship

Research & Frameworks

RIG
Regulatory Interpretation Gap

The RIG framework identifies and characterizes the structural distance between regulatory mandate and technical specification in healthcare software delivery. It provides quality practitioners with a diagnostic model for locating where compliance intent erodes across the translation chain from policy text to acceptance criteria to deployed system behavior.

Core Dimensions

  • Mandate ambiguity detection
  • Specification fidelity scoring
  • Vendor interpretation variance
  • Regulatory traceability mapping
ATL
Acceptance Translation Layer

The ATL framework defines a structured mediation layer between regulatory intent and executable acceptance criteria. It formalizes the processes by which quality governance teams translate policy requirements into verifiable test conditions, preserving compliance intent across multi-vendor and multi-release delivery environments.

Core Dimensions

  • Criteria formalization patterns
  • Cross-vendor normalization
  • Acceptance chain traceability
  • Release-gate governance protocols

Additional Research Areas

Multi-Vendor UAT Governance Architecture

Structural models for coordinating user acceptance testing across heterogeneous vendor ecosystems where contractual obligations, technical interfaces, and quality standards diverge. Focuses on governance roles, escalation protocols, and consolidated acceptance signoff mechanisms.

Release Assurance in Regulated Environments

Gate-based release governance frameworks for healthcare IT programs subject to CMS, HIPAA, and ACA compliance requirements. Examines the relationship between quality metrics, compliance evidence, and release authorization authority.

Software Quality Leadership in Healthcare Platforms

Practitioner perspectives on building and sustaining quality engineering functions within regulated health plan platforms — including team capability models, QA maturity assessment, and the organizational positioning of quality governance relative to delivery and compliance functions.

Infrastructure Automation Quality Assurance

Testing and governance approaches for infrastructure-as-code pipelines in cloud-native healthcare environments, with focus on Azure DevOps, Terraform, and Ansible-based deployment automation within security-bounded architectures.
