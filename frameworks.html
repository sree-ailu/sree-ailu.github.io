Original Research Frameworks

Frameworks

Four original governance frameworks developed from frontline experience in regulated, multi-vendor healthcare software delivery.

RIG
Regulatory Interpretation Gap

Diagnoses the structural distance between regulatory mandate and technical specification, locating where compliance intent erodes across the translation chain.

Core Dimensions

  • Mandate ambiguity detection
  • Specification fidelity scoring
  • Vendor interpretation variance
  • Regulatory traceability mapping
Maturity

Defined and documented; formal peer review in progress

Future

AI-assisted interpretation alignment extension

ATL
Acceptance Translation Layer

Formalizes the mediation layer between regulatory intent and executable acceptance criteria across multi-vendor delivery environments.

Core Dimensions

  • Criteria formalization patterns
  • Cross-vendor normalization
  • Acceptance chain traceability
  • Release-gate governance protocols
Maturity

Defined and documented; formal peer review in progress

Future

Extension into a scored governance model

TCR
Testability Coverage Rate

A metric and scoring methodology quantifying the testable proportion of system behavior — distinct from test-pass rate or code coverage.

Core Dimensions

  • Testable-behavior identification
  • Coverage-vs-testability scoring
  • System-level aggregation
  • Industry-track measurement model
Maturity

Initial definition published, QRS 2026 Industry Track

Future

Empirical validation against production incident data

RRG
Release Readiness Governance

Identifies five recurring failure patterns between UAT sign-off and release stability, and proposes a governance checklist separating "tested" from "ready to release."

Core Dimensions

  • Failure-pattern classification
  • Readiness-vs-completion distinction
  • Governance checklist protocol
  • Post-UAT stability signals
Maturity

Articulated in practitioner publication and conference talk (CAST 2026)

Future

Formalization into a scored model for academic submission
